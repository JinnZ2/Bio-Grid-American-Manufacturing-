## Transforming Desertification into Global Computing Infrastructure and Climate Refuge

## Executive Summary

Desertification affects 1.5 billion people globally and is accelerating with climate change. Rather than viewing this as purely destructive, the Desert Energy Sanctuary Framework captures the massive energy flows created during ecosystem transition to power both global digital infrastructure and humanitarian refugee support. AI-coordinated energy harvesting from sun-silica interactions, alkalinity gradients, static electricity, and thermal cycling creates economically sustainable sanctuaries that fund their own humanitarian operations.

**Core Innovation**: Desert energy harvesting powers data centers that generate revenue to support climate refugee communities, creating economic incentives for land restoration while providing technological infrastructure and human sanctuary during climate transitions.
